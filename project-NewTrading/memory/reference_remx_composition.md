**REMX (VanEck Rare Earth & *Strategic Metals* ETF) is NOT a pure rare-earth play.**
By weight it is ~30-40% **lithium** and only partly rare earth. Composition (June 2026, 28 holdings):

- **Lithium sleeve (~30-40%):** PLS (Pilbara), ALB (Albemarle), LTR (Liontown), SQM, 1772 Ganfeng
- **Rare-earth sleeve:** LYC (Lynas), MP Materials, 600111 China Northern Rare Earth
- **Other strategic metals:** 600549 Xiamen Tungsten, 601958 Jinduicheng Molybdenum

**Why it matters:** on any given day the lithium sleeve can dominate the print, so a "rare-earth ETF crash" headline is often a **lithium event in disguise**. On 2026-06-05 REMX fell -8.65% from BOTH sleeves at once (lithium spot rollover + rare-earth scarcity-premium unwind) — see `Trades/REMX_lithium_crash_analysis_20260605.md`.

**Cleaner expressions if the thesis is actually rare earths:**
- **MP / LYC outright** (the at-scale Western/ex-China producers)
- **REXC** — Sprott Rare Earths **Ex-China** ETF, launched 2026-04-15 (holdings Lynas, MP, Arafura). Purer, but newer/thinner — check liquidity before sizing. NOTE: ex-China purity *concentrates* scarcity-premium risk (these names get hit hardest on any Trump-Xi export-control thaw), it does not hedge it.

**Two method lessons from the session this came from:**
1. **Pull actual constituent weights before attributing a basket move to the fund's named sector.** Don't trust the ticker's category label. Relates to [[feedback_anchor_net_claims_on_reconciliation]].
2. **A holdings table's "Chg %" is usually a 1-MONTH column — do NOT read it as the daily move.** Mistook +4.27% 1M on MP as "green today" when MP was actually -9.6% on the day.
