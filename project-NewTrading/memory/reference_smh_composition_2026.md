**SMH (VanEck Semiconductor ETF) composition as of 2026-05-27:**
- NVDA weight dropped from **~20% → ~8%** vs the prior basket
- Move = basket is materially more diversified across the semi complex; single-stock event risk lowered

**Implication for trading SMH:**
- Single-stock earnings events (NVDA, AMD, AVGO, ASML, TSM, INTC) now have proportionally smaller impact on SMH vol
- "NVDA earnings inside the expiry" is no longer a near-binary thesis-killer the way it was when NVDA dominated the basket
- Parabolic moves in SMH are now broader-semis-driven, not NVDA-driven — supports basket-exhaustion theses over single-name squeeze theses
- Compare to 2026-04-24 SMH bull plan (`Trades/SMH_trade_20260424.md`) where NVDA earnings calendar drove the expiry choice — that constraint has loosened

**How to apply:**
- Before flagging single-stock event risk on ANY ETF trade (SMH, XLK, XLF, etc.), verify current top-holding weights — they drift faster than feels intuitive
- For SMH specifically, the 2026-05-27 weights make the basket behave more like a broad semi index than an NVDA proxy
- Watch for further composition drift; ETF weights are dynamic and this is a moving target
